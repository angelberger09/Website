# Homepage shadow and organization lessons

Timestamp: 2026-06-25 22:15 ET  
Source feedback: FEEDBACK-019, FEEDBACK-020

## LESSON-027 — Strengthen lifted-paper shadows across the homepage

Status: active

### Context

Screenshot review of the live homepage showed that the paper/card system needs more visible physical separation at screenshot scale.

### Lesson

Homepage paper/photo pieces should use visible lifted-paper depth, not faint or purely theoretical shadows. Header tabs, route slips, paper cards, section fields, and contact-sheet pieces should cast readable drop shadows or contact shadows that separate them from the cream paper background while preserving text contrast and performance.

### Future rule

When refining homepage paper surfaces, validate the shadow in the rendered browser. If the page still feels flat or washed into the background, add stronger but soft layered shadows, small contact shadows, or sheet offsets before adding more borders or panels.

## LESSON-028 — Make the homepage read as a left-docked paper docket

Status: active

### Context

Screenshot review of the live homepage showed that useful content exists, but the route slips and content boards need clearer human-readable grouping and separation.

### Lesson

The homepage should read as an organized studio docket: left-docked when appropriate, grouped by purpose, separated by calm spacing, and easy to scan. Paper pieces should feel intentionally pinned and sequenced, not randomly scattered across a large cream field.

### Future rule

When refining the homepage layout, group related paper pieces, align them to a clear editorial spine, create visible section separation, and use spacing/shadow/scale to make the reading order obvious. Avoid spreading many small pieces across the screen without a clear hierarchy.
