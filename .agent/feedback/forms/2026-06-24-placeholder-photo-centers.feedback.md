## Public-safe summary

The site needs placeholder photo centers that look like rendered image/shot areas, not blank or generic backing plates. Placeholder does not mean fake finished work; it means a professional, intentional visual stand-in until real gallery, portfolio, or store imagery exists.

## Extracted signal

Use rendered placeholder photos or illustrated shot backgrounds inside polaroid/card centers so the page feels professionally composed. The placeholders should be clearly authored and visually useful, but not pretend that unavailable projects or products are already live.

## Implementation target for later runs

- Add a small set of reusable placeholder shot types, such as studio desk, paper texture close-up, art preview surface, portfolio process table, product prep surface, and notes/notebook scene.
- Use these as background/center fills for photo cards, gallery cards, and hero polaroids.
- Keep placeholders calm, paper-textured, and brand-aligned.
- Avoid fake product listings, fake case studies, or misleading live inventory.

## Do

- Make every placeholder image feel intentionally staged.
- Use soft paper, studio, art, and object-photo language.
- Label or contextualize placeholders when the surrounding content is not live yet.

## Avoid

- Flat gray blocks.
- Empty transparent centers.
- Decorative photos that do not support the page meaning.
- Placeholder imagery that falsely implies live products or completed case studies.
