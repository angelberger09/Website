# Soft Chaos Studio Style Tile Spec

Working brand guide for visual direction, website foundation, and the first Comfort Oddlings product line.

## Brand Snapshot

Soft Chaos Studio is a cozy, emotionally aware artist brand built around handmade-feeling art, soft color, gentle chaos, and tiny comfort creatures. The work should feel safe, slightly weird, warm, and relatable without becoming childish, overly polished, minimalist, luxury, or clinical.

- Core feeling: seen, safe, cozy, comforting, a little funny
- Audience: late teens through late 20s; soft, strange, healing humans; colorful weirdos; people who like art that feels personal
- World structure: Oddlings are the broad creature world. Comfort Gonks are one subgroup inside that world.
- First launch direction: character-focused digital mini packs; line art version + colorful sticker version

## Color Palette: Soft Cottage Rainbow

Use warm cream and sage as the main foundation. Let coral, lavender, and dusty cornflower blue carry the soft rainbow feel. Butter yellow and seafoam mint should be used as small pops rather than full-page anchors.

- Warm Cream `#F6EFE4`: landing page background; soft page foundation
- Soft Sage `#A8B79A`: primary brand anchor; buttons, panels, gentle emphasis
- Mushroom Beige `#D9CCBE`: neutral support; cards, borders, quiet backgrounds
- Peach Coral `#FFB08F`: warm secondary accent; blush, flowers, highlights
- Lavender `#C8B6D9`: soft emotional accent; headers, stickers, cozy details
- Dusty Cornflower Blue `#9EB6D9`: cool balance; links, waves, calm details
- Butter Yellow `#F3D77A`: small joy pop; stars, buttons, tiny emphasis
- Seafoam Mint `#99DEC9`: fresh pop; sea glass, waves, playful accents
- Warm Cocoa Brown `#4C3B31`: primary text and linework instead of black

## Typography

- Grandstander: brand title, major page headlines, and style tile section headers
- Quicksand: body copy, product descriptions, labels, forms, navigation, and small sticker-sheet notes

## Texture, Line Style, and Illustration Rules

- Texture mood: warm handmade paper background; optional crochet or fiber accents; glossy resin shine only as a small accent
- Line style: thin, neat linework in warm cocoa brown; avoid pure black unless needed for accessibility or production constraints
- Detail level: medium detail; designs should still read cleanly at about 1 inch and scale up to 8.5 inches without getting muddy
- Shape language: rounded, soft, blob-like comfort creatures with accessories; Comfort Gonks specifically keep hidden eyes, big noses, and big hats
- SVG/print direction: clean silhouettes, limited tiny internal details, separated color regions so designs can become SVGs, stickers, coloring pages, and printables
- Avoid: childish, perfect, luxury/minimal, religious motifs, loose glitter, overcomplicated linework, or sad-only presentation

## Core Motifs

Primary motifs for the tile and first website pass: mushrooms, flowers, butterflies, tiny cottages and village shapes, teacups or mugs, sea glass, waves, and dragonflies.

## First Character Direction: Watchful Friend

Watchful Friend is the first likely mini-pack focus. The character should feel like a soft, neutral-leaning blob friend: alert, protective, tender, and trying hard. Keep the base character color consistent across sticker versions so the character becomes recognizable.

- Creature shape: rounded blob friend; not a gonk for this first character
- Base color: soft neutral leaning color from the cream / mushroom / sage family
- Accessories: book with papers fluttering out, paint brushes, palette, cozy mug, tiny notes, or soft nest objects
- Product format: mini pack with two versions: line art pack for coloring/creative use and full-color pack for ready-to-use stickers
- Text approach: mostly character-only at launch. Short phrases can be separate sticker pieces or product description copy

## Website Priority

Start with a warm cream landing page. Other pages can eventually use different soft palette backgrounds, but the first site pass should prioritize clarity, calm, and a welcoming world introduction.

- Home / Landing Page: introduce the studio, tagline, Oddlings world, featured products, and a gentle call to explore
- About: gently weave in the artist story: art as grounding, grief, health, mental health, and rebuilding
- Gallery: show creature art, downloads, sticker sheets, coloring pages, experiments, and mockups
- Field Notes / Studio Notes: process posts about materials, behind-the-scenes making, product updates, and creative work
- Healing Corner: reflective posts about mental health, grief, hysterectomy journey, chronic illness, and healing

## Naming Bank

Starter names: Watchful Friend, Spark Keeper, Firelight Friend, Dino Keeper, and Shell Keeper.

Supporting name bank: Fern Friend, Mossy Keeper, Little Fossil Friend, Pebbleback Pal, Bloom Buddy, Garden Keeper, and Sea Glass Friend.
