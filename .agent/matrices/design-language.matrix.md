# Design Language Matrix

| Design ID | Token or Pattern | Meaning | Current Use | Lesson Source | Do | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| DESIGN-001 | Warm Cream Background | Soft studio atmosphere | `body`, `.home-page` | LESSON-001 | Keep page calm and warm | Harsh white or dark technical shell |
| DESIGN-002 | Large Editorial Type | Authored identity | Scroll title and section heading | LESSON-001 | Let type create the arrival moment | Overfilling with explanatory copy |
| DESIGN-003 | Rounded Minimal Cards | Former gentle card base to refine into paper pieces | Page destination cards and shared card primitives | LESSON-003, LESSON-022 | Use only as historical context; current shared cards should move away from rounded corners | Dense dashboard cards or smooth rounded UI panels |
| DESIGN-004 | Floating Header | Persistent personal anchor | Top header | LESSON-002, LESSON-020 | Keep `Angel Berger` visible and make the surface feel like paper over the page | Replacing identity with repo language or making the header read as generic glass UI |
| DESIGN-005 | Sage Accent | Soft active marker | pills and folder mark | LESSON-001 | Use sparingly | Too many competing accents |
| DESIGN-006 | Human-Facing Copy | Friendly orientation | homepage text and card descriptions | LESSON-002, LESSON-023 | Say notes, archive, pages, doors, and short supportive page subtitles | viewer shell, endpoints, deployment test, or oversized page intro prose |
| DESIGN-007 | Gradient Fade-Off Structure | Structure without hard boxes | proposed homepage panels/cards | LESSON-008 | Use soft fade-offs and color fields | Relying on outlines for every separation |
| DESIGN-008 | Flat Cel-Shaded Atmosphere | Graphic depth without heavy 3D | proposed background/shading | LESSON-007 | Use flat highlights and shade shapes | Realistic rendering or glossy skeuomorphism |
| DESIGN-009 | Animated Background Texture | Subtle life and motion | proposed background layer | LESSON-006 | Keep motion calm and intentional | Random busy particles |
| DESIGN-010 | Tactile Polaroid Depth | Physical-photo surface feel | proposed cards/hero layers | LESSON-010, LESSON-021 | Use gloss, scratches, slight distortion, layered transparency, and filled polaroid centers when frames become cards | Heavy realistic 3D, over-rendered effects, or empty frame centers masquerading as cards |
| DESIGN-011 | Offscreen Top Emergence | Header feels physically attached to the viewport edge | proposed floating header revision | LESSON-011 | Let the header tuck into or emerge from the top edge while preserving access | Fully hiding controls, clipping focus states, or making navigation feel broken |
| DESIGN-012 | Unboxed Left-Expanding Menu | Navigation feels integrated into the header line | proposed folder menu revision | LESSON-013 | Keep menu links open by default and expand them left of the Pages control | Downward boxed dropdown panel, separate background, or border-heavy overlay |
| DESIGN-013 | Art Lettering Typography | Hero type feels printed, drawn, playful, and studio-made | hero display type and section headings | LESSON-014 | Use softer weight, gentler tracking, and expressive display fallbacks | Overly heavy or cramped headline treatment |
| DESIGN-014 | Paper List Hero Lockup | Welcome title reads like a tactile paper note or list | hero lockup | LESSON-014, LESSON-019 | Use list structure, paper layers, grain, gloss, and subtle rotation | Floating words with no material anchor |
| DESIGN-015 | Direct Unnumbered Navigation | Header nav is clear and immediate | proposed header nav revision | LESSON-015 | Use plain destination labels only | Edge numbers, helper labels, metadata, or redundant triggers |
| DESIGN-016 | Soft/Sharp Balance | Brand remains gentle but not mushy | proposed visual refinement | LESSON-015 | Add contrast, firmness, and hierarchy where interaction needs clarity | Making every surface equally soft, hazy, or low-contrast |
| DESIGN-019 | Paper Material Authenticity | Whole page system reads as handmade paper/collage | shared page heroes, cards, backgrounds, filled sections, About paper boards, and polaroid motifs | LESSON-019 | Show fiber, torn/cut edges, layered paper shadows, scanned imperfections, collage overlaps, and taped board/note compositions while preserving readability | Generic translucent glass panels, isolated paper motifs, or softness that hides material character |
| DESIGN-020 | Paper Header Surface | Top bar reads as a paper strip laid over the page | shared floating header in `app/paper-card-pass.css` and `app/paper-header-pass.css` | LESSON-020, FEEDBACK-011 | Make the whole header system visibly papery at screenshot scale: the strip, identity side, and nav labels should use paper texture, cut/tab silhouettes, slight edge irregularity, existing material assets or shader-like CSS, and a visible soft drop shadow | Smooth glass capsule, overly blurred nav bar, pill-like nav chips, hidden focus states, shadow so faint the header floats without contact, or new header systems that bypass the existing pass |
| DESIGN-021 | Content-Bearing Polaroid Cards | Polaroid frames become usable content surfaces | homepage hero polaroid card in `app/page.jsx` and `app/hero-polaroid-card-pass.module.css`; future project/product cards | LESSON-021 | Fill the transparent center with readable content, preview art, route cues, or page information while keeping the cream frame and dark backing tactile | Leaving polaroids as empty background decor when they are meant to act as cards |
| DESIGN-022 | Paper Piece Card Surfaces | Shared cards read as individual paper or polaroid pieces | page cards, detail cards, source links, wide cards, reader cards, pathway rows, next-step bands, and About principles/pathway note boards | LESSON-022, LESSON-024 | Reduce uniform rounding, add cut/uneven edge cues, use layered backing shadows, show texture, and keep content inside the paper surface | Generic rounded translucent cards, pillowy panels, or card shapes that do not feel like physical paper pieces |
| DESIGN-023 | Supportive Page Subtitles | Page intro titles orient quietly instead of acting as hero display text | `PageIntro` titles across About, Notes, Portfolio, Store, and similar page routes | LESSON-023 | Use 2–5 words, thinner weight, slightly smaller scale, and one-line fit | Treating every page intro as oversized hero text or letting subtitles wrap into multiple lines |
| DESIGN-024 | Torn Edge Card Silhouettes | Shared cards look like cut or torn paper sheets | `app/paper-card-pass.css` shared card surfaces and `app/about-paper-collage-pass.css` About note boards | LESSON-024 | Use `border-radius: 0`, cut-paper clip paths, edge shadows, layered offsets, and paper texture | Rounded card corners, pillowy cards, or mobile fallbacks that reintroduce rounded UI-card silhouettes |
| DESIGN-025 | Route Photo-Center Cards | Shared page intro polaroids carry route-specific visual material | `PageIntro` in `app/site-chrome.jsx` with `.page-hero__photo-card` in `app/globals.css` | LESSON-025 | Fill each route intro photo center with short, honest, route-specific labels and visual surface treatment | Empty shared polaroid spans, fake screenshots, or imagery that implies unavailable products/case studies |
| DESIGN-026 | Section Paper Fields | Large sections need grounded paper/collage background presence | Store availability and About paper-board sections | FEEDBACK-012 | Use layered paper fields, tonal blocks, sheet offsets, tape cues, and texture to ground content | Washed-out blank fields or adding more opaque app containers |
| DESIGN-027 | Grainy Root Paper | The environment surface should read as paper before cards appear | `body`, `.site-shell`, `.atmosphere-layer` | FEEDBACK-013 | Make grain/fiber visible at the root while maintaining contrast | Smooth digital pastel backgrounds or texture so strong it reduces legibility |
| DESIGN-028 | Crinkled Paper Material | Paper should feel lightly handled and imperfect | shared material surfaces | FEEDBACK-014 | Use soft crease cues, pressure variation, tonal wrinkles, and scanned imperfections | Harsh dirty realism, unreadable wrinkles, or pristine flat panels |
| DESIGN-029 | Flattened Paper Hierarchy | Hierarchy should feel editorial, not nested app UI | filled page sections, wide cards, Store sections, and About principles/pathways | FEEDBACK-015 | Use paper placement, snippets, spacing, and composition instead of card-within-card nesting | Dashboard-like panels, repeated nested boxes, or ornamental hierarchy with no purpose |
| DESIGN-030 | Text Paper Snippets | Text lives on small paper notes, labels, strips, or snippets | headings, labels, supporting copy, route guidance, Store notes, and About principle/pathway notes | FEEDBACK-016 | Break appropriate copy into readable paper fragments | Giant generic text panels or tiny unreadable scrap labels |
| DESIGN-031 | Photo-Led Content Surfaces | Visual/photo surfaces carry content structure with supporting text | Store preview board in `app/store/page.jsx` and `app/store-paper-snippet-pass.css`, Portfolio, PageIntro, gallery, and preview areas | FEEDBACK-017 | Use honest image-like surfaces and staged visuals without implying fake products or completed work | Text-only panels for everything, empty photo decals, or misleading product/case-study imagery |
| DESIGN-032 | Placed Availability Paper Strips | Availability rows feel like placed collage strips rather than UI rows | Store availability states and repeated wide bands | FEEDBACK-018 | Stagger, vary, or break rows into physical paper-strip/snippet compositions | Long repeated app-list rows with only paper styling applied |
| DESIGN-033 | Alternating Scroll Gallery | Gallery experiences become scrollable paper/photo sequences | `app/portfolio/page.jsx`, `app/portfolio-gallery-pass.css` | LESSON-026 | Alternate image-forward cards with text-note cards, use native horizontal scroll, preserve readable captions, and keep imagery honest to available public data | Static decorative background images, fake screenshots, dense carousel chrome, hidden controls, or image cards that imply unavailable work |
