## Public-safe summary

Photo-card centers need authored shot backgrounds so they look like professional visual material. The center of a photo card should feel staged, composed, and connected to the page, not like a placeholder rectangle or a dark backing layer.

## Extracted signal

For a polished Website, each photo/polaroid area needs an intentional internal scene: paper texture, art object, notes surface, product prep surface, project detail, or soft studio background. The shot background should make the card look finished even before final source imagery is available.

## Implementation target for later runs

- Define a small library of placeholder shot backgrounds.
- Pair each Website route with a suitable shot type.
- Use internal image fills and object-fit/object-position rules so the photo center feels composed.
- Add subtle image treatment that matches the paper/collage system: grain, muted contrast, soft shadow, and frame-safe cropping.
- Keep text on top of images only when contrast is clearly readable.

## Do

- Use professional composition and cropped internal scenes.
- Keep shot backgrounds calm and brand-aligned.
- Make the center support the surrounding page story.

## Avoid

- Blank gray/dark panels.
- Random decorative imagery.
- Image fills that make text unreadable.
- Overly glossy stock-photo language that fights the handmade paper direction.
