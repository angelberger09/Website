## Public-safe summary

The Website should add a new gallery pattern that uses internal scrollable images with an alternating rhythm, instead of leaving photo material as static background decoration.

## Extracted signal

A gallery should feel like a series of paper/photo pieces visitors can move through. The rhythm should alternate between image-forward cards and small text/context cards so the gallery feels authored, professional, and alive without becoming a dashboard.

## Implementation target for later runs

- Create a scrollable gallery module that can appear on the homepage or relevant Portfolio/Store sections.
- Use alternating image-card and text-snippet rhythm.
- Let images sit inside paper/polaroid surfaces with textured frames and staged centers.
- Keep controls simple: native horizontal scroll, visible overflow hint, keyboard/focus support, and reduced-motion-safe behavior.
- Use route-specific placeholder images until live gallery assets exist.

## Do

- Alternate visual and text surfaces.
- Make each image tile feel like a placed photo or paper piece.
- Keep captions short and human-facing.
- Preserve accessible focus and scroll behavior.

## Avoid

- One static background decal trying to carry the whole page.
- A dense carousel with hidden controls.
- Image-only clutter with no route meaning.
- Fake gallery content that pretends unavailable work is complete.
